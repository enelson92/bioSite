Lori's Hobbies

Between a booming real-estate career and caring for her family as a single mother, Lori keeps a busy schedule. However, she still makes time to do things that she enjoys. Whether she is going down the street for ice cream, planning a cruise vacation, or making a cross-country trip to the West Coast, she always makes sure that her family is the focus, and that they are always a part of it.

Web Page Inspiration

Colors

Lori’s favorite color is purple, which can have both light and dark hues and shades. When I asked her to describe herself in a color, she said purple because it matches her personality. Therefore, I wanted to pay homage to this by choosing these hues for my color scheme.

Fonts

Because Lori is a real estate agent, I wanted to design the website that was influenced by the tone of the real estate industry. Because real estate involves hundreds of thousands (if not millions of) dollars, I wanted to make sure the fonts used in the font design included serif, as the tone of the fonts creates more of a sophisticated feel, while still maintaining a sense of modernism.

Web Page Structure

I was influenced by other real estate website designs. Overall, a majority of websites are designed in a simple way. This makes the site not only user-friendly, but provides the sense that the agent is taking care of the client in its simplicity. I really enjoyed this website from Luxury Presence Real Estate, due to its simple design and contrasting colors. Instead of the black background, I intend on using a light pink/purple background color for the body of the website. Additionally, I like having the header be visible to the user at all times, no matter where you are on the page.

Website Tone/Background Photos

For any background photos for the header or body, I wanted to include a picture of a pristine house or neighborhood. When interviewing Lori, I really enjoyed how she said that she’s not a salesperson; rather, she is a matchmaker to find your dream home. Therefore, I wanted to use images that reflected this “dream-like” tone of what “home” might feel like.
